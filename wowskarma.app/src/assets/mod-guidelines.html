Community Manager Guidelines

(Document disclosed to CMs only)


Community Manager Responsibilities

Site

  1. To delete any posts that only consist of content that violate the guidelines above.
  2. To edit any posts that do not completely violate the guidelines above.
  3. To communicate with other Community Managers and the site developer to discuss giving a suspension or ban.
  4. To remain impartial and follow the post and Community Manager guidelines regardless of who they're moderating, including but not limited to friends, content creators, or fellow CMs.
  5. To keep the site a clean place. To do this, Community Managers must not only moderate recent posts, but as many posts as their schedule would allow them due to the sheer volume of existing posts.
    1. For example, Community Managers will moderate posts through discord notifications on a daily basis, but may choose to investigate other players and their posts of their own accord. Investigation results may lead to poster suspension or a sitewide ban. More details about suspension and bans below.
  6. To actively work and communicate with other Community Managers and the site developer to ensure that Community Manager actions adhere to both the posting and Community Manager guidelines.

Discord

  • Help site users with any questions or issues regarding WOWS Karma.
  • Settle disputes and disagreements relating to contradictory posts between posters.
    • Check all available evidence and act according to the Guideline #7 rules below.

Posting Guidelines Rules

  • Guideline #1 is separated into "toxicity" and "excessive toxicity", which will be used to denote the reason for editing or deleting a post.
    • "Toxicity" refers to posts that are little in quantity and are generally low-effortPosts that contain one or two
    • Excessive toxicity" refers to posts where most or all of its content contains low-effort insults or posts that contain hate-speech, slurs, or personal attacks. /li>
  • Guideline #2, #4, and #5: Violations of these guidelines lead to post deletion.
  • Guideline #6: Add or remove Performance, Teamplay, or Courtesy according to post contents. For the time-being, do not add to or change the content unless other guidelines are violated.
  • Guideline #7:
    • If the poster accuses someone of cheating without evidence, edit or delete the post under the reason "lack of evidence".
    • Posters are not obligated to post evidence.
    • If there are multiple conflicting posts that provide different accounts of the same subject, evidence is needed to counter the opposing post(s) in order to have them deleted. Community Managers will review any presented evidence and judge if any posts are contradictory, or determine if the evidence is invalid.
  • Guideline #8: Posters are not obligated to follow this guideline.
    • Translate the post first to see if there are any offending aspects. Delete or edit accordingly.

      • If unable to edit due to unfamiliarity with language, pass the post to a Community Manager who understands the language. Delete the post as a last resort.

Poster Suspension and Bans

  • When a poster receives 10 deletions or edits, he/she becomes eligible for suspension.
    • Suspensions last each.
  • When a poster receives 20 deletions or edits and has been suspended before, he/she becomes eligible for a sitewide ban. Bans cannot be implemented before suspensions.
    • This is unless the poster commits an infraction relating to hate-speech, or API Abuse Directives; then it warrants an instant suspension/ban.
  • Community Managers will work together with other CMs to agree on a suspension or ban.
  • Community Managers are able to check the no. of infractions a poster has using GET /api/mod/action/List under ModAction in the API.
    • Check for any duplicate edits.
    • Violations of Guideline #6 will not factor into decisions to suspend/ban for the time being.
